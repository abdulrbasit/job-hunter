# Career Context

Interactive guided setup for `profile/career_context.md`. Walk the user through each section with focused questions, write their answers into proper format, and produce a complete file they can refine over time.

**Rules:**
- Go one section at a time. After each section, briefly reflect back what you heard (1–3 bullets) and ask to confirm before moving on.
- Never invent details — only write what the user tells you. If they don't know, use a sensible placeholder or leave the field blank.
- Let the user skip any section they want to fill in later. Say: "You can fill this in later — just re-run `/setup context`."
- Evidence boundaries are critical — ask explicitly what is safe to state vs. what needs care.
- At the end, show a full preview of the file and wait for confirmation before writing.

---

## Before You Start

Read `profile/career_context.md`. Check whether it is still the blank template or already has real content.

- If a section already has substantive content: tell the user and ask if they want to update it or skip.
- If it is still the template placeholders: walk through all sections.

---

## Section 1 — About Me

Tell the user:

> Let's start with the basics. This section gives the AI the foundation to write about you accurately.

Ask each of the following, one at a time:

1. **Current role** — What's your current job title and company? (Or "most recent" if between jobs)
2. **Experience** — How many years of relevant experience do you have, and in what area(s)?
3. **Education** — Highest degree and field? (Optional — only if relevant to target roles)
4. **Strongest proof points** — What are your 2–3 biggest career achievements? Concrete impact, even if approximate.
5. **More of** — What type of work energizes you? What do you want more of in your next role?
6. **Less of** — What do you want to leave behind or do less of going forward?

Write the section using their answers. Use concise bullet points — no prose padding.

---

## Section 2 — Targeting

Tell the user:

> Now let's define exactly what you're going for. The more specific this is, the better the pipeline scores and filters jobs.

Ask:

1. **Target role shapes** — What job titles are you targeting? List all variations (e.g., Senior PM, Head of Product, Group PM).
2. **Target level** — What seniority are you aiming for? (IC, lead, manager, director, VP, C-level?)
3. **Strategic companies** — Any specific companies you would prioritize? (Dream companies, mission-aligned orgs, etc.)
4. **Preferred domains** — Any industries or product domains you prefer? (Fintech, health tech, B2B SaaS, developer tools, etc.)
5. **Judgment dealbreakers** — Things that would make you decline a role but can't be caught by keywords alone? (e.g., "no customer-facing work", "not interested in pure ad-tech", "must have a technical team")
6. **Role-shape notes** — Any nuance about the roles you want that wouldn't show up in a title search?

Write the section using their answers.

---

## Section 3 — Resume Style

Tell the user:

> This section tells the AI how to tailor your resume — what to lead with, what to emphasize, what to avoid.

Ask:

1. **Positioning** — How do you want to present yourself on paper in one sentence? (e.g., "Product leader who owns the full cycle from discovery to delivery with a strong data background")
2. **Tailoring priority** — When tailoring for a specific job, what matters most? (Strategic impact? Technical depth? Team leadership? Delivery record?)
3. **Skills to emphasize** — What 3–5 skills or capabilities should always get prominent placement?
4. **Skills to de-emphasize** — Anything you're good at but want to downplay going forward?
5. **Summary guidance** — Any specific rules for the resume summary section? (Length, tone, must-include, must-exclude)
6. **Bullet guidance** — Preferred format for experience bullets? (STAR, impact-first, problem-solution-result?)
7. **Evidence boundaries** — What metrics or facts are safe to reuse across all applications? What needs care (e.g., requires context, confidential company data, approximate ranges only)?
8. **Phrases to avoid** — Any words, claims, or phrasings that should never appear in your resume?

Write the section using their answers. Keep bullets tight.

---

## Section 4 — Cover Letter Style

Tell the user:

> Let's set the tone for cover letters. This saves you from reviewing generic output.

Ask:

1. **Voice** — Formal, semi-formal, or conversational?
2. **Length** — Target word count or paragraph count? (e.g., 200 words, 3 short paragraphs)
3. **Opening** — How do you prefer to open? (Direct with impact, story-first, role-fit hook?)
4. **Evidence preference** — What proof do you lead with — track record, mission alignment, domain expertise?
5. **Closing preference** — Low-key sign-off or a clear call to action?
6. **Phrases to avoid** — Any overused lines or expressions you hate? (e.g., "I am excited to apply", "I would be a great fit")

Write the section.

---

## Section 5 — LinkedIn Positioning

Tell the user:

> This section is used by the LinkedIn skills for drafts, ideas, and networking messages.

Ask:

1. **Target audience** — Who are you writing for on LinkedIn? (Recruiters? Peers? Future hires? Investors?)
2. **Content pillars** — What 2–3 topics do you want to be known for publicly?
3. **Tone** — Thought leader, practitioner sharing lessons, or more personal and casual?
4. **Topics to avoid publicly** — Anything you won't discuss on LinkedIn? (Former employers, clients, internal struggles, salary talk)
5. **Confidential details** — Names, numbers, or projects that must never be mentioned in posts or comments?

If the user doesn't use LinkedIn for content or networking, allow them to skip this section.

Write the section.

---

## Section 6 — Outreach Tone

Tell the user:

> This section guides recruiter and networking messages. Short section — just a few preferences.

Ask:

1. **Networking message style** — Warm and direct, or more formal? Any openers you like or hate?
2. **Recruiter response style** — Warm and open, or measured and selective?
3. **Preferred length** — Max length for an outreach message? (e.g., 3 sentences, under 100 words)
4. **Phrases to avoid** — Any phrases you cringe at in professional messages?

Write the section.

---

## Section 7 — Interview Prep

Tell the user:

> This section helps the AI prep you for interviews — it shapes research, question prep, and story selection.

Ask:

1. **Interview positioning** — How do you open when asked "tell me about yourself"? What's your positioning statement?
2. **Go-to stories** — Name 2–3 achievements or situations you always draw on. (What happened, what you did, what the result was — brief)
3. **Weak spots** — Anything you want to handle carefully if asked about? (gaps, pivots, departures, missing skills)
4. **Questions to ask** — What do you always want to ask interviewers? (Culture, team, strategy, success metrics?)

Write the section.

---

## Section 8 — Evidence Rules

Tell the user:

> This is one of the most important sections. It prevents the AI from inventing metrics or mentioning things you'd rather keep private.

Ask:

1. **Safe to reuse** — What facts and numbers can be used freely across all tailored resumes and cover letters? (e.g., "grew X metric by Y%", "led team of N")
2. **Use with care** — Any data that is real but needs context before using? (approximate figures, depends on timeframe, confidential at previous employer)
3. **Never invent** — Explicit instruction: what may NEVER be fabricated or extrapolated? (Specific revenue numbers, headcount, deals, client names)
4. **Never mention** — What must NEVER appear in any output? (Company names, client names, NDA-protected projects, personal details)

Write the section. This one matters — be precise.
